# Literature Review Notes

This folder contains public literature review notes on AI-assisted pediatric vision screening, amblyopia, strabismus, healthcare AI validation, and real-world deployment challenges.

## Purpose

The purpose of these notes is to organize key themes from publicly available research and identify broad challenges in the field.

## Key Themes

### 1. Pediatric Vision Screening

Early detection of pediatric vision conditions is important because delayed identification may affect long-term visual development and treatment outcomes.

### 2. AI-Assisted Screening

Recent research has explored the use of artificial intelligence, computer vision, and smartphone-based imaging to support pediatric eye screening.

### 3. Real-World Validation

Many healthcare AI systems show promising performance in controlled research environments, but real-world deployment introduces additional challenges such as image quality variation, device differences, workflow constraints, and user variability.

### 4. Dataset Generalizability

Dataset diversity, population representation, imaging conditions, and external validation are important considerations for responsible healthcare AI development.

### 5. Accessibility and Implementation

Scalable screening approaches require attention to accessibility, usability, clinical workflow integration, and equity in healthcare delivery.
